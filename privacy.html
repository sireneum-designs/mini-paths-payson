how we handle what you share

Privacy & data use

The Mini Paths of Payson is a community art and research project. Every postcard, drawing, and path on the map was given to us by a person. Here's exactly what we do with that — and what we will never do.

Our pledge

We will never sell your data. We will never share it with advertisers, brokers, or any third party for marketing. We will only ever contact you about The Mini Paths of Payson — nothing else.

01 — what we collect

When you submit a postcard or path

You decide how much to share. The only thing we actually need is the path or postcard itself. Everything else is optional.

  • Your path — the route you drew on the map (geographic coordinates).
  • Your postcard — the drawing, photo, or words you sent in.
  • How it makes you feel — the feeling word and any "why" you wrote.
  • Optional details — your name (or just a first name, or nothing), your age, and an email if you want us to send you the link to the live map.
02 — how we use it

For the project, and only the project

Your contributions appear on this map and in connected exhibits, reports, and conversations about Payson's everyday geography of connection, peace, joy, and nature. That's it.

  • Public: your postcard art, the path you drew, the feeling word, and (if you signed it) your first name & age.
  • Private: your email, if you gave one. It's never shown publicly and is used only to send you the link to the project.
  • Approval first: every submission is reviewed before it appears on the public map.
03 — where your data lives

Stored simply, kept safe

Submissions live in a database hosted by Supabase, with images stored in their secure storage layer. Access is limited to the project organizers. We don't use third-party analytics, ad trackers, or fingerprinting cookies.

04 — kids & families

A note for parents

Some of our most beautiful postcards come from children. When a young person submits a postcard at a community event, a parent or guardian is present. We display only first names (or initials, or no name at all) by default. If you'd like a child's submission anonymized further or removed, see below — we'll handle it the same day we hear from you.

05 — your rights

You can ask us to remove your data, anytime

You're the author of your contribution. If you want it taken off the public map, anonymized, or deleted from our database entirely, just say so — no explanation needed.

Request removal

Email us at the address below. We'll confirm we received it, do the removal, and email you back when it's done — usually within a few days.
